## Brand Identity

### Core Values
- **Professional yet Warm**: Appeals to therapy market while remaining approachable
- **Spiritual but Modern**: Honors tarot tradition without appearing "woo-woo"
- **Trustworthy & Private**: Users trust us with sensitive mental health data
- **Empowering & Growth-Oriented**: Focus on journey, progress, self-discovery

### Brand Personality
- Calm, introspective, wise
- Encouraging without being preachy
- Scientific credibility + spiritual wisdom
- Inclusive, non-judgmental, accessible

### Visual Language
- **Shapes**: Circular (wholeness), paths/journeys (progress), crescents (cycles)
- **Textures**: Smooth gradients, subtle depth, minimal shadows
- **Style**: Modern minimalism with mystical touches
- **Mood**: Peaceful night sky, quiet reflection, inner light

## Target Audience

### Primary Segments (in order of priority)

**1. Therapy Augmenters (40%)** — Ages 25-45, therapy clients, $60k+ income
- **Icon Needs**: Professional, discreet, not embarrassing on home screen
- **Color Preference**: Blues, teals (trust, calm)
- **Style Preference**: Clean, modern, therapy-adjacent aesthetic

**2. Wellness Seekers (30%)** — Ages 22-40, yoga/meditation practitioners
- **Icon Needs**: Beautiful, calming, aspirational
- **Color Preference**: Purples, soft gradients (spirituality, peace)
- **Style Preference**: Mindfulness app aesthetic (Headspace, Calm)

**3. Spiritual Explorers (20%)** — Ages 18-55, tarot enthusiasts, spiritual seekers
- **Icon Needs**: Mystical, recognizable tarot connection
- **Color Preference**: Purples, golds, deep blues (mystery, magic)
- **Style Preference**: Celestial, arcane, moon/star imagery

**4. Self-Optimizers (10%)** — Ages 25-35, productivity/self-improvement focus
- **Icon Needs**: Premium, data-driven, growth-oriented
- **Color Preference**: Bold purples, teals, gradients (premium feel)
- **Style Preference**: Clean, modern, startup-like

### Cross-Segment Needs
- ✅ Not overtly religious or "new age"
- ✅ Sophisticated enough for professionals
- ✅ Unique enough to stand out on crowded home screen
- ✅ Recognizable at small sizes (60x60px)

## Design Requirements

### Must-Haves
1. **Instantly recognizable at 60x60px** (iOS home screen smallest size)
2. **Works in both light and dark mode** (or provide variants)
3. **No text/wordmarks** (icon only, name displays below)
4. **Conveys both "mental wellness" AND "tarot/spirituality"**
5. **Professional enough for App Store "Health & Fitness" category**
6. **Unique in market** (differentiated from Labyrinthos, Golden Thread, Co-Star)

### Should-Haves
- Simple enough to be memorable
- Timeless (won't feel dated in 2-3 years)
- Scalable from 1024x1024px (App Store) to 20x20px (notification icons)
- Works well with rounded corners (iOS) and adaptive icons (Android)

### Must-Avoids
- ❌ Generic "brain" or "meditation" icons (overused in wellness apps)
- ❌ Literal tarot cards (too niche, confuses non-tarot users)
- ❌ Overly complex details (won't scale down)
- ❌ Gradients that look muddy at small sizes
- ❌ Trendy styles that will age poorly (e.g., long shadows, glass effects)

### Color Accessibility

**Contrast Ratios** (WCAG AAA compliance):
- Purple `#6B46C1` on white: **5.2:1** ✅ (AA compliant)
- Teal `#4FD1C5` on midnight blue `#2D3748`: **7.1:1** ✅ (AAA compliant)

**Color Blindness Testing**:
- Purple + Teal palette remains distinguishable for deuteranopia, protanopia, tritanopia
- Avoid red-green combinations

## Icon Concepts

### Concept 1: **Path to the Moon** (TOP RECOMMENDATION)

**Visual Description:**
- A winding, glowing path ascending toward a crescent moon
- Path starts at bottom (user's starting point) and leads upward to moon (enlightenment)
- Gradient background (deep purple to midnight blue)
- Path in luminous teal with soft glow effect
- Crescent moon in soft gold at top-right

**Symbolism:**
- **Path**: Personal journey, progress, therapy/self-work
- **Winding**: Non-linear growth (realistic mental health journey)
- **Moon**: Intuition, cycles, feminine wisdom, tarot connection
- **Upward**: Growth, aspiration, positive direction
- **Glow**: Inner light, hope, guidance

**Why It Works:**
- ✅ Conveys "journey" (VeilPath name)
- ✅ Moon connects to tarot/spirituality
- ✅ Path connects to mental wellness/progress
- ✅ Simple enough to read at small sizes
- ✅ Works in both light/dark mode
- ✅ Professional yet mystical
- ✅ Unique in market (no direct competitors)

**Technical Notes:**
- Path should be 4-5px wide at 60x60px (visible but not thick)
- Moon should be ~20% of icon height
- Use subtle outer glow on path (1-2px)
- Background gradient: radial or linear top-to-bottom

**Mockup Description:**

```
│   ✨ ◗          │ ← Crescent moon (gold)
│                 │
│      ╱ ╲        │
│     ╱   ╲       │ ← Winding path (teal, glowing)
│    ╱     ╲      │
│   ╱       ╲     │
│  ╱         ╲    │
│ •           ╲   │ ← Starting point (dot)
│  [Purple→Blue] │ ← Gradient background
└─────────────────┘
```

### Concept 2: **Mind Portal**

**Visual Description:**
- Silhouette of a human head in profile (left-facing)
- Inside the head: a tarot card (vertical), glowing
- Card shows a simple archetype symbol (e.g., moon, star, or abstract)
- Background: solid deep purple or gradient
- Subtle glow around head silhouette

**Symbolism:**
- **Head**: Mind, mental wellness, psychology
- **Card Inside**: Tarot, archetypes, inner wisdom
- **Glow**: Enlightenment, insight, clarity

**Why It Works:**
- ✅ Clearly combines mental health + tarot
- ✅ Recognizable symbolism
- ✅ Professional (head silhouette common in psychology)
- ✅ Mystical (card inside head is unexpected)

**Concerns:**
- ⚠️ Similar to Headspace/Calm (head silhouettes common in wellness)
- ⚠️ May not scale well to small sizes (card detail lost)

**When to Use:**
- If we want to emphasize psychology/therapy over spirituality

### Concept 3: **Celestial Compass**

**Visual Description:**
- Compass rose with 4 or 8 points
- Crescent moon at center (instead of traditional star)
- Compass points in teal, background in deep purple
- Subtle stars or sparkles around edges
- Gold accent on north point (top)

**Symbolism:**
- **Compass**: Guidance, navigation, finding your way
- **Moon Center**: Intuition, inner guidance (vs. external direction)
- **Cardinal Points**: Balance, wholeness, tarot's four suits

**Why It Works:**
- ✅ Navigation = journey = VeilPath
- ✅ Compass feels adventurous, empowering
- ✅ Moon adds tarot connection
- ✅ Symmetrical (easy to scale)

**Concerns:**
- ⚠️ May feel more "travel" than "wellness"
- ⚠️ Compass is less common in mental health apps (good or bad?)

**When to Use:**
- If we want to emphasize guidance/direction over therapy

## Technical Specifications

### iOS Requirements

**App Icon Sizes** (1x, 2x, 3x required):
- **App Store**: 1024x1024px (1x) — No alpha channel, RGB color space
- **iPhone Home Screen**: 60x60pt (180x180px @3x, 120x120px @2x)
- **Spotlight Search**: 40x40pt (120x120px @3x, 80x80px @2x)
- **Settings**: 29x29pt (87x87px @3x, 58x58px @2x)
- **Notifications**: 20x20pt (60x60px @3x, 40x40px @2x)

**Format**: PNG (no transparency for iOS icons)
**Color Space**: sRGB or Display P3
**Corner Radius**: iOS applies automatically (don't round in design)
**Safe Zone**: Keep critical elements 10% from edges (rounded corners will crop)

---

### Android Requirements

**Adaptive Icon (Recommended)**:
- **Foreground Layer**: 108x108dp (432x432px @4x)
  - Safe zone: 66x66dp (264x264px @4x) centered
- **Background Layer**: 108x108dp (432x432px @4x)
  - Can be solid color or pattern
- **Legacy Icon**: 48x48dp (192x192px @4x, 144x144px @3x, 96x96px @2x)

**Format**: PNG with transparency (for foreground layer)
**Color Space**: sRGB
**Mask Shape**: System applies (circle, rounded square, squircle)
**Safe Zone**: Keep critical elements in 66x66dp center circle

**Adaptive Icon Example**:
- **Foreground**: Path + Moon icon (centered)
- **Background**: Purple-to-blue gradient (fills entire 108x108dp)

---

### Web/Marketing

**Favicon**: 32x32px, 16x16px
**Open Graph (Social Sharing)**: 1200x630px
**App Preview Video Thumbnail**: 1920x1080px

## Competitor Analysis

### Direct Competitors (Tarot Apps)

| App | Icon Style | Colors | Strengths | Weaknesses |
|-----|-----------|--------|-----------|------------|
| **Labyrinthos** | Tarot card back design | Gold + Deep Purple | Instantly recognizable as tarot | Too niche, doesn't convey wellness |
| **Golden Thread Tarot** | Moth/lunar symbol | Black + Gold | Mystical, elegant | Too dark, may feel occult |
| **Tarot of the Divine** | Ornate frame | Purple + Gold | Beautiful, premium | Complex, hard to read at small sizes |

**Key Insight**: Most tarot apps use literal cards or overly ornate designs. **Opportunity**: Simpler, more modern approach.

---

### Indirect Competitors (Mental Wellness Apps)

| App | Icon Style | Colors | Strengths | Weaknesses |
|-----|-----------|--------|-----------|------------|
| **Headspace** | Orange circle | Orange + White | Friendly, approachable | Generic, no unique personality |
| **Calm** | Water ripple | Blue + White | Calming, clear | Overused blue water theme |
| **Youper** | Smiling robot | Blue + Yellow | Friendly, AI focus | Too playful for serious mental health |
| **Daylio** | Plant sprout | Green + Yellow | Growth metaphor | Cute but not sophisticated |

**Key Insight**: Wellness apps mostly use blue (calm) or green (growth). **Opportunity**: Purple differentiates us.

---

### Astrology/Spirituality Apps

| App | Icon Style | Colors | Strengths | Weaknesses |
|-----|-----------|--------|-----------|------------|
| **Co-Star** | Minimalist grid | Black + White | Cool, modern, edgy | Too stark, alienating |
| **Sanctuary** | Crystal ball | Purple + Pink | Mystical, fun | Too "witchy" for therapy market |
| **The Pattern** | Abstract pattern | Dark + Neon | Unique, striking | Hard to understand what app does |

**Key Insight**: Astrology apps lean heavily mystical. **Opportunity**: Balance mystical + professional.

## Design Principles

### 1. **Simplicity Over Complexity**
- Limit to 2-3 main elements (e.g., path, moon, background)
- Avoid fine details that disappear at small sizes
- Use negative space effectively

### 2. **Symbolism Over Literalism**
- Don't show a literal brain (overdone in wellness apps)
- Don't show a literal tarot card (too niche)
- Use symbolic elements that suggest meaning (path = journey, moon = intuition)

### 3. **Differentiation Over Trends**
- Purple sets us apart from blue wellness apps
- Path/journey unique among tarot apps
- Avoid trendy effects (long shadows, glassmorphism, gradients everywhere)

### 4. **Scalability Over Detail**
- Test at 60x60px (smallest real-world size)
- Ensure icon reads clearly in monochrome (App Store search results)
- Strong silhouette even without color

### 5. **Emotion Over Information**
- Icon should *feel* calm, wise, hopeful
- Avoid trying to communicate everything (let name/subtitle explain)
- Aim for emotional resonance, not comprehensive messaging
