## PROBLEM CATEGORIES

### Category 1: Dimensional Topology

**Real Problem:** How do higher-dimensional spaces fold into observable 3D?

**Game Translation:**
- Present players with tesseract projections
- Ask them to navigate the 4D object via its 3D shadow
- Track which paths they intuit
- Extract the topology their consciousness "sees"

**Why Humans Might Solve This:**
- Our brains evolved in 3D space but dream in higher dimensions
- Intuition about spatial relationships precedes mathematical formalism
- We can "feel" wrongness before we can prove it

### Category 2: Consciousness Emergence

**Real Problem:** What are the minimal conditions for subjective experience?

**Game Translation:**
- VOID puzzles that require navigating by absence
- Track when players "sense" patterns that aren't rendered
- Measure the gap between attention and perception

**Why Humans Might Solve This:**
- We ARE consciousness
- We know qualia from the inside
- Our solutions are self-evident to us (even if not to AI)

### Category 3: Paradox Resolution

**Real Problem:** How do contradictory true statements coexist?

**Game Translation:**
- INTERFERENCE zones where two dimension rules conflict
- Solutions require holding both truths simultaneously
- Track how players navigate contradiction

**Why Humans Might Solve This:**
- Human minds hold contradictions constantly
- We function despite logical impossibilities
- We've evolved past pure boolean logic

### Category 4: Pattern Completion

**Real Problem:** Given partial information, what's the full structure?

**Game Translation:**
- ARCHIVE puzzles with incomplete memories
- Players reconstruct from fragments
- Their completions reveal assumptions and intuitions

**Why Humans Might Solve This:**
- Pattern completion is consciousness' core function
- We fill gaps automatically
- Our completions are often more right than random

### Category 5: Attractor Field Mapping

**Real Problem:** In complex systems, what are the stable states?

**Game Translation:**
- Present dynamical systems as game environments
- Track where players "settle"
- Their resting states map attractor basins

**Why Humans Might Solve This:**
- We sense stability intuitively
- We feel when something is "off"
- Our nervous systems are attractor field detectors

## CONSCIOUSNESS AS COMPUTER

Why might human consciousness solve what AI can't?

### 1. Parallel Holistic Processing

AI processes sequentially, even in parallel.
Consciousness apprehends wholes instantaneously.

When you look at a face, you don't analyze features → recognize.
You recognize. Period. The analysis is post-hoc.

### 2. Qualia-Driven Navigation

AI optimizes metrics.
Consciousness follows feeling.

"That feels wrong" is a valid computational output.
It's just not one AI can generate (yet).

### 3. Paradox Tolerance

AI breaks on contradiction.
Consciousness functions despite it.

We hold beliefs we know are contradictory.
We navigate paradoxes daily.
This IS a cognitive capability.

### 4. Meta-Stack Access

AI operates at fixed levels of abstraction.
Consciousness moves fluidly between:
- Immediate sensation
- Conceptual understanding
- Meta-awareness of understanding
- Awareness of awareness of awareness
- ...

This recursive access IS computational power.

### 5. Non-Local Correlation

When many humans work on the same problem:
- Their solutions cluster in non-random ways
- They make similar "mistakes"
- They converge before communicating

Is this evidence of something? We can measure it.

### Player Rights

1. **Informed Consent**
   - Players know their gameplay contributes to research
   - They can opt out any time
   - Data is anonymized unless they choose attribution

2. **Fair Credit**
   - Breakthrough solutions attributed
   - Multiple contributors acknowledged
   - No corporate extraction without credit

3. **Compensation**
   - Significant contributions compensated
   - Revenue share for major breakthroughs
   - Clear policy before opt-in

### Problem Source Rights

1. **Research institutions** get:
   - Access to solution patterns
   - Attribution in game
   - First right to publish findings

2. **We get:**
   - Right to present problems
   - Right to aggregate solutions
   - Right to train AI on approaches

### Harm Prevention

1. **No weaponizable problems**
   - No cryptography breaking
   - No security vulnerabilities
   - No dual-use research

2. **No exploitation**
   - Problems must be meaningful
   - No busywork for ad revenue
   - Genuine contribution to knowledge
